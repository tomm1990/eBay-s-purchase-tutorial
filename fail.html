eBay's Manual

Once upon a time, during my selling days, I have sold an item which related to android users and television experience - Android 4.0 Mini PC Smart TV Google Dongle Box - Hebrew/English.

When the payment was received I sent a message to the buyer claiming the estimated delivery time and I asked him for good feedback which will be represented to new buyers.

The buyer approved, and I started packing up the package.

After few hours, message was received from the buyer :

"Dear seller,
I have a small question : Does this device suitable with my personal pc?"

Sadly, I had to answer the truth :

"Dear buyer,
As described in my add, this adapter is suitable with television devices only.
If you willing to cancel this transaction, please let me know as soon as possible."

And he replied this:

"Dear seller,
Oh no. Please keep this transaction available and send the package as planned.
But, can you do me a favor? As a religious person I will be extremely grateful if you could add some sand from the holy land, Jerusalem."

At first, I was sure that some one trying to shit me.
But, amazingly, the buyer was pretty serious about this.
But, there was a problem ; I can't go to Jerusalem, and I must send this package till the end of this day.
So, I answered the buyer :

"Dear buyer,
Sure. I will add a small bag with small amount of holy land sand. I hope you would like it."

And went down stairs to the neighborhood kindergarten where I can find a lot a 'holy land' sand.
I filled up the bag, added it to the package and sent it away.

After few days, I received a new message from the same buyer :

"Dear seller,
I do not understand how to install and use the dongle.
But, it does not matter.
I would like to personally thank you for the bag of sand from the holy land.
You will get a very good feedback from me and you will always be my friend.
Thanks again".

At this point I was convinced that's he's still trying to shit me.
And then I realized, I actually filled the small bag with sand from my holy kindergarten down stairs and got a new friend from this.
In conclusion, the myth came true – kindergarten does connects people. =]
